# ISO 27001:2022 — Operational guidance

The 2022 revision restructures Annex A from 114 controls (4 categories) into **93 controls organized in 4 themes**: Organizational, People, Physical, Technological. This skill is the operational pointer; canonical content in `ontologies/iso27001-2022/annex-a.ttl`.

## Structure

### Main standard clauses (4-10) — ISMS requirements
- 4. Context of the organization
- 5. Leadership
- 6. Planning (risk assessment + treatment plan)
- 7. Support (resources, competence, awareness, comms, doc)
- 8. Operation
- 9. Performance evaluation (monitoring, internal audit, mgmt review)
- 10. Improvement (nonconformity, corrective action, continual improvement)

Clauses are MANDATORY for certification. They become Policies (N1) and Procedures (N2) in the pyramid.

## Key invariants

1. **Statement of Applicability (SoA)** — declares which Annex A controls apply, justification for inclusion/exclusion. MANDATORY for certification. Audit-readiness rule `A_ISO27_02` enforces SoA presence.
2. **Risk register linked to controls** — every implemented control must trace to risks it mitigates (rule `A_ISO27_04`).
3. **Evidence trail per control** — operational evidence (logs, KPIs, audit records) must be linkable per control (rule `A_ISO27_03`).
4. **PDCA improvement** — Clause 10 requires continual improvement. Each KPI's `pdca_loop` field is the tactical implementation.

## Generation patterns

### Per-control coverage minimum
For each in-scope control, the pyramid must produce:
- 1 Policy section (or full Policy) addressing the control objective
- 1 Procedure with operational steps
- BPMN if the control is process-driven (most are)
- ≥1 KPI measuring effectiveness

### Frequent ETI scope subsets

Most ETI clients scope ISO 27001 to:
- All A.5 (Organizational) — non-negotiable
- A.6.3, A.6.7 (People — awareness, remote working)
- A.7.1, A.7.4 (Physical — premises, monitoring)
- A.8 selectively: A.8.1-12 (endpoints + config + data), A.8.16 (monitoring), A.8.20-23 (network + filtering), A.8.32 (change)

Out-of-scope frequently: dedicated facility controls if hosted (A.7.5-13), cryptographic-heavy A.8.24 if outsourced.

## Audit-readiness extras

Beyond the generic checks (`A_ISO27_01-05`), specific to 27001:2022:
- SoA must be signed by management AND dated < 12 months
- Internal audit cycle ≥ annual (Clause 9.2)
- Management review minutes archived (Clause 9.3)
- Risk treatment plan present and current (Clause 8.3)
