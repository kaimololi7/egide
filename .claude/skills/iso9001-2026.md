# ISO 9001:2026 — Operational guidance

ISO 9001:2026 is the planned 2026 revision of ISO 9001:2015 (still in draft as of writing — the FDIS). Major focus areas: explicit climate change consideration (Clause 4), strengthened risk-based thinking, more explicit process approach. This skill is the trigger framework for our prioritized persona — **responsable qualité ISO certifié**.

## Structure (PDCA + 10 clauses)

| Clause | Focus | PDCA |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 | Scope, references, terms | — |
| 4. Context of the organization | Stakeholders, scope, QMS, processes | — |
| 5. Leadership | Top mgmt commitment, policy, roles | Plan |
| 6. Planning | Risks/opportunities, objectives, change planning | Plan |
| 7. Support | Resources, competence, awareness, comms, documented info | Do |
| 8. Operation | Operational planning, requirements, design, production, control | Do |
| 9. Performance evaluation | Monitoring, audit, mgmt review | Check |
| 10. Improvement | Nonconformity, corrective action, continual improvement | Act |

## Process approach (cornerstone of 9001)

The standard requires identifying processes, their **inputs/outputs/interactions/owners/risks**, and their KPIs. This is the **direct semantic match** to our 4-level pyramid:

| ISO 9001 process attribute | Pyramid artifact |
|---|---|
| Process inputs/outputs | BPMN start/end events + data objects |
| Process interactions | BPMN message flows + sub-process calls |
| Process owner | Policy RACI + Procedure §4 actors |
| Process risks | Linked to OSCAL risk register |
| Process KPIs | N4 KPI artifacts |

**Insight**: ISO 9001 is the framework most natively aligned with our pyramid. Selling to a quality manager is selling them a tool that produces exactly what they need to maintain.

## New / strengthened in 2026 vs 2015

1. **Climate change context** — Clause 4 now requires explicit consideration of climate change as a relevant context factor (decision from ISO IAF 2024).
2. **Stronger process approach** — interactions between processes more explicitly required.
3. **Risk-based thinking** — embedded in every clause, not just §6.
4. **Documented information** — same flexible approach as 2015 (no procedural docs required by name, but evidence required).

## Key invariants

1. **Customer focus drives KPIs** (Clause 5.1.2) — at least one KPI per pyramid must measure customer-relevant outcome (audit-readiness rule `A_ISO9_04`).
2. **PDCA in every KPI** — `pdca_loop` field non-empty (rule `A_ISO9_05`).
3. **Management review ≥ annual** — documented (rule `A_ISO9_06`).
4. **Process interactions documented** — pyramid must show how N3 BPMNs link to other BPMNs in/out (call activities, message flows).

## Frequent ETI scope

ETI quality managers typically scope to:
- All clauses 4-10 (mandatory for certification)
- Core production / service processes (5-15 BPMNs)
- Customer-facing processes (sales, support, complaints)
- Internal audit + management review processes
